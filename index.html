Bhagavad Gita 2.47

"You have a right to perform your prescribed duty, but you are not entitled to the fruits of action. Never consider yourself to be the cause of the results of your activities, nor be attached to inaction."

Karmanye vadhikaraste, Ma phaleshu kadachana,
Ma karma phala hetur bhurma, Te sangostvakarmani.

Interpretation: Focus on your duty without attachment to results. Perform actions as an offering, free from expectations.

Isha Upanishad 1

"The whole universe is pervaded by the Divine. Everything animate or inanimate that is in the universe is controlled and owned by the Lord. One should therefore accept only what is necessary, which is set aside as one's quota, and not accept other things, knowing well to whom they belong."

Īśāvāsyam idaṁ sarvaṁ yat kiñca jagatyāṁ jagat,
Tena tyaktena bhuñjīthā mā gṛdhaḥ kasya svid dhanam.

Interpretation: Recognize the divine presence in all things. Take only what you need, without greed, understanding everything belongs to the Supreme.
